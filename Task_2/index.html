Who was Dr. Kalam?

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

He was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts. He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India.

Timeline of Greatness

  • 1931: Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960: Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist after studying aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1980: Successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit, putting India on the global space map.
  • 1990s: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO. He played a major role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • 1997: Honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India. He was widely referred to as the "People's President".
  • 2007: Left office and became a visiting professor at several prestigious institutes including IIM Shillong and IIM Ahmedabad.
  • 2015: While delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, he collapsed and passed away from an apparent cardiac arrest on July 27.
